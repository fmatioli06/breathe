Wk 1–2
You'll feel it immediately
Sleep quality improves fast, especially with extended exhale before bed. Stress response during the day feels more manageable. With the 5.5 breath, you'll notice a calmer baseline — your nervous system responds to breathing changes within a single session.
Wk 2–4
Physical markers start shifting
Resting heart rate begins dropping. If you wear a WHOOP or Garmin, HRV scores will visibly trend upward. Nasal congestion often clears as CO₂ tolerance improves and your nose starts doing its job again.
Wk 4–6
Athletic performance changes
Buteyko work starts paying off. Your Control Pause score climbs. You'll sustain nasal-only breathing at higher CrossFit intensities. Recovery between sets feels faster. Nestor notes endurance athletes see measurable aerobic efficiency gains around this window.
Wk 6–12
Structural changes set in
CO₂ tolerance is significantly rebuilt. You're no longer over-breathing at rest. Inflammatory markers are lower with consistent Tummo practice. Slower, more efficient breathing becomes automatic even when you're not thinking about it.
Biggest accelerator — mouth taping at night
Nestor credits mouth taping as the single fastest way to accelerate all of these gains. Forcing nasal breathing during sleep — when you can't self-correct — is where most structural adaptation happens. Use a small piece of surgical tape or SomniFix strips. His own 10-day experiment produced dramatic improvements in snoring, blood pressure, and sleep quality. If you try one thing outside the sessions, this is it.
